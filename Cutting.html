What is Cutting

Cutting

Cutting refers to a phase or approach where individuals focus on reducing body fat while preserving lean muscle mass to achieve a lean and defined physique. During the cutting phase, the primary goal is to create a calorie deficit by consuming fewer calories than the body needs, typically through a combination of reduced calorie intake and increased calorie expenditure through exercise.

What Foods should you eat when Cutting

Egg Whites

Being a low-fat protein source, egg whites aid in muscle preservation during cutting as they are high in protein and contain essential amino acids necessary for muscle repair and growth, while keeping the fat content low

Salmon

Salmon are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and protein, salmon is a fish option that promotes heart health, aids in muscle retention during cutting, and supports overall health by providing essential nutrients, supporting cardiovascular function, and combating inflammation.

Chicken Breasts

Chicken Breasts is a lean protein source that aids in muscle maintenance during a calorie deficit because it provides essential amino acids necessary for muscle repair and growth while being relatively low in fat.

Greek Yogurt

Greek yogurt is a high-protein dairy option that supports muscle retention during cutting due to its rich protein content, which helps prevent muscle breakdown and provides a satisfying snack option with fewer calories than some other snacks.

Tuna

Tuna is a low-fat fish, tuna is packed with protein and omega-3 fatty acids, making it an excellent choice for cutting as it supports muscle preservation and cardiovascular health while being relatively low in calories and fat.

Broccoli

Broccoli is a low-calorie vegetable packed with fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making it an ideal choice for cutting as it promotes satiety, aids in digestion, and provides essential nutrients without adding significant calories.

Spinach

Spinach is a nutrient-dense leafy green, spinach is rich in iron and antioxidants, which support overall health and well-being during cutting by providing essential nutrients and combating oxidative stress.

Cauliflower

Cauliflower is a low-calorie vegetable that is rich in vitamins and fiber, making it suitable for creating satisfying, low-calorie meals during cutting, helping to create a calorie deficit without sacrificing satiety or nutrient intake.

Cottage Cheese

Cottage cheese is a protein-rich option that promotes muscle maintenance and satiety during cutting because it provides a slow-release source of protein (casein) that helps prevent muscle breakdown and keeps you feeling full for longer.

Turkey Breasts

Turkey breast is a lean protein source that aids in muscle preservation while providing essential nutrients during cutting, being low in fat and high in protein, supporting muscle growth and repair without adding excessive calories.

Sweet potatoes

Sweet potatoes are a complex carbohydrate source, sweet potatoes provide sustained energy during cutting and contribute to satisfying meals due to their fiber content, helping to control hunger and maintain stable blood sugar levels.

Green beans

Green beans are low-calorie vegetables packed with nutrients and fiber, green beans are beneficial during cutting as they support overall health and digestion, while their fiber content promotes a feeling of fullness and helps control calorie intake.

Asparagus

Quinoa is a whole grain with high protein and fiber content, making it an ideal choice for sustained energy and satiety during cutting, helping to regulate blood sugar levels and keep hunger at bay.

Lentils

Brussels sprouts
